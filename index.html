✦ The story being told ✦

This wedding is not a single moment.

It is a conversation between the living and the gone — between what was carried forward and what is being made new. Every thread was chosen deliberately. Every stone, every stitch, every flame is part of a language that has been building itself quietly for years.

On December 5th, it speaks.

✦
✦ The ancestry of the day

Every Thread, Traced

A wedding is a story told in objects. Each one carried to the altar for a reason — some spoken, some silent, some known only to the one wearing them.

The DressHer mother's gown, reborn

Not preserved behind glass but worn, altered, made present again. Transformation as an act of love.

The EarringsHer aunt's opalite

Carried by a woman she never met, now carried by her. Her mother knows. That is enough — and it is everything.

The Tiara & NecklaceNew, in the same stone

New pieces chosen in opalite — the same shimmer as the earrings she inherited. Old and new speaking the same language.

Something BorrowedFrom Houston's people

A piece from those no longer here on his side. Wearing it says: I am joining all of you, the present and the gone alike.

The Flower GirlHouston's petals

She scatters petals from every flower he ever gave her. Time itself walks down the aisle first.

The LanternCarried, not clutched

Gold frame, living flame, plum and emerald ribbon. She walks toward him with light in her hands instead of flowers.
